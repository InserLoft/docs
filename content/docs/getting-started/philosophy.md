Inserloft's guiding principle is simple:

> Build the future of AI infrastructure.

This means Inserloft optimizes for the ecosystem, not for any single flagship product. A few consequences follow from that:

**Infrastructure over one-off models.** A model is only as useful as the platform, APIs, and tooling around it. Inserloft invests in Pixel, the APIs, and the SDKs as much as it invests in training runs.

**Efficiency has a place next to scale.** The Kyro frontier models push capability forward, while the Baby LLM family focuses on models that are small, fast, and cheap enough to run locally. Both are treated as first-class goals, not as a "smaller version" of the same thing.

**Open source accelerates adoption.** Verty, Inserloft's first open-weight model, is released under the Apache License 2.0 specifically because permissive licensing encourages commercial use and wider adoption. OpenGit exists to keep publishing in that spirit.

**Developers are the multiplier.** Research produces models, the Platform makes them usable, and Developers turn that into products companies actually run. See the [Roadmap](/docs/getting-started/roadmap) for how these stages connect.
