Inserloft's roadmap follows a consistent pipeline, from research to real-world usage:

```
Research
   │
   ▼
Models
   │
   ▼
Platform
   │
   ▼
Developers
   │
   ▼
Companies
```

**Research** explores architectures, training methods, alignment, and inference techniques.

**Models** packages that research into named, versioned releases — the Kyro frontier family and the Verty open-weight family.

**Platform** (Pixel) is where those models become usable: uploaded, versioned, documented, and organized into projects.

**Developers** consume the platform through APIs, SDKs, and the CLI to build real applications, including Cleo itself.

**Companies** adopt what developers build, closing the loop and feeding new requirements back into Research.

Notable upcoming milestones referenced in this documentation include **Kyro 3.5**, planned for September 2026, representing the next step up in scale and capability for the Kyro frontier family.
